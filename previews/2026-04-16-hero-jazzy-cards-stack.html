On the studio
Three rooms, one desk, a lacquer cutter that's older than the building. Everything a record needs and nothing it doesn't.
01 · Live to tape
Studio A

Two-inch, sixteen-track, no edits.

1,200 sq ft, 22-foot ceilings, a floated floor over oak. What goes to tape is what happened in the room — nothing gets edited into shape later.

Up to 12 musiciansAvailable from May 2026
02 · Hybrid mastering
Room B

Analog front end, digital trust.

A Studer A80 through a Manley Variable Mu, summed down to digital stems. We cut a reference lacquer in-house for every full-length before it ships to pressing.

Stems or stereoLacquers included
03 · Field sessions
On location

The room is the instrument.

Porch, pier, parish hall, kitchen table. We travel with ribbons, a Nagra IV-S, and a kettle — and we don't touch a mix until we're back at the desk.

US & Gulf onlyBookings 2 weeks out
